About Malay College Kuala Kangsar

Malay College Kuala Kangsar (MCKK) is a premier all-boys boarding school in Perak, Malaysia, established in 1905. Often referred to as the "Eton of the East," it was originally founded to educate the sons of Malay royalty and nobility. Today, MCKK is renowned for its academic excellence, leadership development, and strong traditions in sports and extracurricular achievements. The college continues to produce influential leaders across various fields in Malaysia and maintains a prestigious reputation nationally and internationally.

In addition, I was a member of Badan Nahdhah Islamiah (BANI), where I engaged in values-based leadership and spiritual development programs. My involvement in BANI further strengthened my communication skills, teamwork, and sense of responsibility. Overall, my experience at MCKK equipped me with a strong academic foundation, competitive spirit, and leadership mindset that prepared me to thrive in higher education and future professional environments.

Safe Car Vent

The "Safe Car Vent" project utilizes an Arduino Uno to enhance in-car safety and comfort by automatically managing air circulation. The system monitors temperature and carbon monoxide levels using sensors, activating a fan and vent mechanism when either exceeds safe thresholds. This project aims to prevent hazardous conditions inside the vehicle, particularly during hot weather or in enclosed spaces, ensuring a safer environment for passengers and reducing the risk of carbon monoxide buildup.
